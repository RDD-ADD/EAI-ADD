Retrieval-Based Deep Difference Modeling for Audio Deepfake Detection

RDD-ADD

Abstract

With the rapid advancement of ultra-realistic audio deepfakes, Audio Deepfake Detection (ADD) has raised serious attention. In recent years, retrieval-based augmentation technology has shown great potential in enhancing ADD by providing additional contextual information. However, existing methods rely on a single bonafide audio database, which limits deep difference learning. We propose Retrieval-based Deep Difference Modeling for ADD (RDD-ADD), integrating dual-database retrieval, anchor-based similarity fusion, and GRU-based contextual modeling to enhance representational power and capture fine-grained differences between bonafide and spoof audios. Experimental results show that RDD-ADD significantly outperforms baselines on multiple benchmarks.
